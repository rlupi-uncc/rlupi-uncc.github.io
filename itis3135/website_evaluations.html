Website Evaluations

Stripe

Stripe is a modern payments/fintech website with a strong focus on clarity, readable docs, and consistent UI components.

  • Contrast — Even with the colorful backgound and design, maintains readabliity.
  • Repetition — Repeated component styles, icons, and consistent spacing across pages.
  • Alignment — Grid-based layout where content and controls align predictably.
  • Proximity — Related information is grouped together, and certain items stick to keep them near relevant information

Overall: Follows CRAP well — clear typography and spacing make content scannable. Clean and repeated UI patterns help with muscle memory and learning the product.

art.yale.edu

Art.yale has poor organization, lack of cohesive color scheme, many competing visual elements, and a very busy page.

  • Contrast — Background has too much color variation, and the page has too many conflicting colors.
  • Repetition — Very little consistent repetition of typography/controls. Many different styles are mixed together with a lack of cohesion.
  • Alignment — Elements of the screen are very cluttered on the left side, and the right side remains completely empty.
  • Proximity — Lots of unrelated items packed together with no real direction as to what you might find where.

Overall: Breaks almost all possible design rules, creating a confusing, unpleasant experience to view.
